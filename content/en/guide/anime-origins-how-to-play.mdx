<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding Rarity, Summons, and Trait Rerolling">

Building a powerful roster is the core mechanic of the game. At launch, the banner features highly coveted mythic units such as Yuta (Cursed Lover) and Meliodas. While legendaries can carry you through the initial stages, mythics possess unique multi-stage abilities and massive stat scaling that are required for late-game content.

Once you secure a mythic unit, you must optimize their traits and stats. Traits are passive modifiers that can completely redefine a unit's role in battle. By using trait reroll tokens in the lobby, you can cycle through options until you land a high-tier passive.

| Trait | Rarity | Main Effect |
|--- |--- |--- |
| **Immortal** | Secret (0.1%) | Restores power at 0 stacks, instantly kills nearby targets, knocks back/slows |
| **Ascendant** | Mythic | Massive overall stat multiplier and combat efficiency boost |
| **Hustler** | Common | Increases wave income based on placement |
| **Decay** | Rare | Applies damage-over-time (DoT) poison to targets |

</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Combat and Placement Walkthrough">

Once your team is assembled, it is time to deploy. The combat system relies on strategic placement, resource management, and understanding enemy pathways. Follow these steps to clear your first story stages successfully.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Map Layout">
    Examine the enemy path, spawn points, and placement zones. Identify optimal choke points where your ground units can maximize their area-of-effect (AoE) attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy Cheap Ground Units First">
    Place cost-effective ground units like Naruto or Toji near the front of the track. Naruto uses his Nine Tails Cloak passive to ramp up damage, while Toji offers high base penetration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Set Up Status Effect Units">
    Deploy support units such as Shinobu along straight pathways. Her poison dash inflicts continuous damage-over-time, softening up high-health targets before they reach your main defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Place Hill and Hybrid Units">
    Position high-ground units like Akeno on elevated platforms. Ensure their range covers both ground pathways and the airspace to neutralize incoming flying enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Initiate Upgrades and Skill Copies">
    As waves progress, spend accumulated cash to upgrade your active units. If using evolved Yuta, utilize his copy ability to replicate powerful active skills from your teammates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Ground** | Trackside | Frontline damage, blocking, physical slashes | First / Strongest |
| **Hill** | Elevated Platforms | Ranged support, aerial defense, elemental AoE | Flying / Fast |
| **Support** | Corners / Straights | Poisoning, slowing, economy generation | First |

<Callout type="info" title="Flying Unit Alert">
Do not attempt advanced story maps or raids without at least one dedicated hill unit like Akeno or Aneko. Standard ground units cannot target flying enemies, leading to an instant defeat on later waves.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Progressing Through Story Mode, Raids, and Challenges">

The core progression of the game is split between Story Mode, Raids, and the challenging Infinity Castle. At launch, players can tackle two main story zones: West City and Karakura Town. Each world features multiple difficulty settings that reward gems, upgrade materials, and evolution remnants.

Once you clear the standard story stages, transition into Raids to obtain exclusive character drops and high-tier evolution items. For example, the Seven Deadly Sins raid offers a chance to obtain powerful characters like Ban.
