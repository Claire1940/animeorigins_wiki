<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Trait System and Reroll Strategies">

The Trait system is arguably the most important mechanic in the game. When you summon a unit, it comes with a random trait that provides passive bonuses. These can range from simple damage increases to game-changing mechanics like reduced cooldowns or increased range. The rarest traits, often sitting at a 0.1% pull rate, feature unique visual effects and massive stat multipliers.

When applying **anime origins tips and tricks** to your roster, you must decide when to spend your reroll tokens. It is generally advised to save these tokens for Mythic units or those you intend to evolve into Legends. Rerolling on Epic units is often a waste of resources unless they are essential for a specific early-game strategy.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Combat and Unit Placement">

Combat in Anime Origins is not just about having the strongest units; it is about where you place them and how you manage their upgrades during a match. The game distinguishes between Ground units and Hill units, each serving a specific role in your defensive line.

Effective use of **anime origins tips and tricks** involves creating "kill zones." These are areas on the map where multiple units' ranges overlap, and enemies are slowed down by utility towers. By concentrating your firepower, you maximize the efficiency of your AOE (Area of Effect) attackers.

| Unit Type | Primary Role | Best Placement | Key Upgrade |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ground Melee** | Frontline DPS / Tanking | Corners / Chokepoints | Damage |
| **Ground AOE** | Wave Clear | Middle of straight paths | Range |
| **Hill Sniper** | Boss Damage | High ground / Map center | Attack Speed |
| **Utility Support** | Slow / Stun / Buff | Near the start of kill zones | Cooldown |
