<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Trait Optimization and Reroll Strategy">

Traits are permanent stat modifiers that can turn a "Good" unit into a "God-Tier" one. In **anime origins infinite mode**, the value of a trait is determined by how it helps you handle the scaling health of the enemies. While "Damage" is the most obvious choice, "Cooldown" is often superior for units with slow base attacks.

**Key Traits for Infinite Mode:**
- **Damage Trait**: Increases the power of every hit. Best for units with already fast attack speeds.
- **Cooldown Trait**: Reduces the time between attacks. Vital for "Boss Killers" like Meliodas.
- **Range Trait**: Expands the attack circle. Essential for hill units and map-wide attackers.
- **Secret Trait (0.1%)**: Provides a massive boost to all stats and features a unique activation animation.

| Trait Name | Stat Focus | Best Unit Match | Reroll Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sovereign** | Damage/Range | Yuta Okkotsu / Secret Units | **Highest** |
| **Rapid** | Cooldown | Meliodas (Assault) | **High** |
| **Eagle Eye** | Range | Starrk / Snipers | **Medium** |
| **Warrior** | Balanced | Starter / Mythic Units | **Low** |

<Callout type="info" title="Reroll Resource Management">
Do not waste your limited reroll tokens on units you plan to replace soon. Save your resources for Secret units or units that have already been evolved into their final forms. A 0.1% Secret Trait on a starter unit is a wasted opportunity.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Step-by-Step Infinite Mode Strategy">

Following a consistent placement and upgrade routine is the most reliable way to hit high waves in **anime origins infinite mode**. Use the following steps to optimize your early to mid-game transition.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Wave Setup">
    Place your cheapest high-efficiency unit near the entrance. Do not over-invest here; you only need enough damage to prevent leaks while you set up your economy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Economic Expansion">
    Deploy your economy units or focus on upgrades that increase your money-per-wave. This is the most critical phase for reaching Wave 100+.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Core DPS Placement">
    Once your economy is stable, place your primary DPS (like Yuta) at a central bend. Ensure the unit's range covers as much of the track as possible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mid-Game Upgrading">
    Focus on maxing out one unit at a time rather than spreading upgrades across many weak units. One Max-level Meliodas is better than five Level-1 units.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Support and Utility">
    Around Wave 60, start placing units that provide slows or stuns. This becomes mandatory as enemy health pools become too large to "one-shot."
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Clumping">
While overlapping range is good, clumping all your units in one tiny spot can be dangerous if the game introduces "stun" mechanics or AoE enemy attacks. Spread your heavy hitters across two or three major chokepoints.
</Callout>
